The Importance of Physical Health

When will I have time to exercise? What types of exercises should I do? What type of food should I eat? How do I know if I am having enough sleep? Taking care of your physical body can be a challenge.There are so many questions that we as humans have when it comes to physical health.

To begin with, lets discuss what physical self-care is:

Physical self-care is maintaining your health through physical activities such as walking and eating a healthy diet. It involves putting your body to action. So, in order to maintain a healthy physical body one must begin by making new habits. While making these new habits, one must sacrifice or take out time to do so. One must also persevere and have a set goal.

Your Physical self-care Routine could look like this:

  • To begin, the night before try sleeping by 9:00pm the lastest
  • Once awake, have a healthy breakfast such as an omelet and orange juice.
  • Next, have a set time in your day of at least 30 minutes to exercise.
  • Lunch: Have something healthy such as a Salad or pasta
  • As the day goes on hydrate drinking water
  • During your snack time, eat a fruit or veggie.
  • During dinner time, eat something light like salmon with veggies

Physical Self-care is important in maintaining many things such as: healthy body, healthy weight, immunity, prevent diseases and much more. Furthermore, are three top physical self-care things to do

Exercise

Daily exercise naturally produces stress-relieving hormones in your body and improves your overall health.

  • healthline.com

Food

The food you eat is what gives you energy throughout your day. Eating unprocessed food like fruits, vegetables, and grains is key to keeping you healthy. A healthy diet can help you maintain a healthy weight, reduce your risk of chronic diseases (like heart disease and cancer), and promote your overall health.

  • hhs.gov

Sleep

Adults generally need between seven and nine hours of sleep. While sleeping, your body is repairing, regenerating, and recovering. Lacking sleep can cause lack of concentration, productivity, cognition, low immunity and much more.

  • medicalnewstoday.com
